Oxygen reaches your body via theblood circulation, which carries it from the lungs to the heart and then distributes it throughout the rest of the body.


When we are young, that system works perfectly. But as we age, something changes.

And because of that, your nerves get damaged with poor circulation — and that’s how neuropathy develops.

As we age, our bodyretains less water, meaning there isless fluid in our bloodstreamand blood volume decreases.

And with less blood volume, it becomesmuch harder for the heart to pump enough oxygento all the cells — especially to the extremities, like the feet.


For people with, for example,diabetes, it is even worse.


Ablood sugar level that is too highmakes blood vessels less flexible. They become stiffer, constrict, andfurther restrict blood flow.


Without enough oxygen,nerve cells become damagedand slowly begin to die.
